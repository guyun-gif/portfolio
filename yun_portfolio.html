How I think

My northern stars

Three principles that guide every analysis I do.

01

Business-first

I always start with the "so what" — what does the business actually need? Before I open any tool, I sit down with my client and get clear on what we're trying to move. The numbers come second. The business question always comes first.

02

Engage with data storytelling

Data doesn't speak for itself — someone has to translate it. Whether it's a dashboard, a written report, or a live presentation, I'm always thinking about how to make the insight land. If people walk away confused, the analysis didn't work.

03

Proactive beats Reactive

I don't just execute briefs — I think about them. Some of my best findings were never in the original scope. I spotted something interesting, followed it, and it turned into something useful. Curiosity is what separates good analysis from great analysis.

📖
Hard to read

Heavy data tables made it difficult for non-technical users to extract insight. No visual hierarchy, no comparison context, no way to tell what was good or bad.

↓
UX Design: From data dumps to dashboards people actually use
  • Charts instead of tables — bar charts, trend lines, and visual indicators. The story is readable at a glance.
  • Benchmark comparisons everywhere — every KPI shows YoY, vs. target, or vs. previous period.
  • Hover tooltips for depth — definitions and breakdowns on demand without cluttering the main view.
  • Consistent visual language — standardised colors, layout, and fonts across all 34 dashboards.
⏳
Too slow

The flat data model forced Tableau to process millions of rows at query time. Load times were slow and inconsistent — frustrating every Monday morning.

↓
Star Schema: Ingest only relevant data into Tableau
  • Central fact table surrounded by clean dimension tables.
  • Tableau queries only relevant data — faster loads despite joins.
  • New metrics slot cleanly into the existing model.
  • Easier to maintain and extend over time.
✓ 2–3× faster load times
👻
Invisible pipelines

Stored procedures ran on a virtual machine with no monitoring. Failures were only discovered when someone opened an empty dashboard — often mid-meeting.

↓
ADF Migration: Making the pipeline visible — and reliable
  • Real-time monitoring: see which pipelines ran, when, and whether they succeeded.
  • Instant failure alerts — before anyone opens a dashboard.
  • Structured, searchable error logs. What took hours now takes minutes.
✓ Significantly reduced debugging time

New approach

Focused deep-dives designed to paint a picture of the customer — not just report the numbers.

Your customers come back within a few months on average — but it's a long-tail curve. A significant portion return over a year later for annual replenishment.

Customers come in to buy the signature collection, mostly purchasing 2–3 pieces. The majority keep buying the same collection on their next visit.

Male customers tend to stick to the same fit, while female customers are more likely to follow trends.

Certain markets show notably higher repeat rates among older segments — pointing to distinct loyalty patterns by region.
